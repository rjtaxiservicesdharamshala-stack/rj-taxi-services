Uttarakhand — Where Pilgrimage Meets Paradise

Uttarakhand does not merely host pilgrims. It transforms them. Something happens in these mountains that happens nowhere else in India.

Called Devbhoomi — Land of the Gods — Uttarakhand is home to the holiest rivers, the most sacred shrines, and some of the most spectacular Himalayan landscapes on earth. The Ganges and Yamuna both originate here. The twelve Jyotirlingas include Kedarnath. The Char Dham pilgrimage — completing Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath — is considered the most spiritually meritorious journey a Hindu can undertake.

But Uttarakhand is not only for the devout. Rishikesh — nestled where the Ganges emerges from the Himalayas — is the world\'s yoga capital, drawing seekers from across the globe. Haridwar\'s Ganga Aarti at dusk, with thousands of oil lamps floating on the sacred river, is one of the most visually extraordinary spectacles in India. Mussoorie above Dehradun and Nainital in the Kumaon hills offer colonial-era charm and cool mountain air. Jim Corbett National Park in the Terai foothills is India\'s oldest national park and one of the best places in Asia to spot Bengal tigers.

Our taxi service from Dharamshala places Haridwar and Rishikesh just 5-6 hours away — making Uttarakhand a natural extension of any Himachal Pradesh itinerary. We handle the complete Char Dham circuit with dedicated vehicles, experienced mountain drivers who have made this pilgrimage dozens of times, and complete logistical support.

Char Dham Yatra — 10-Day Sacred Circuit

The most spiritually significant pilgrimage in Hinduism — planned meticulously so every moment is sacred and every logistic is seamless.

Day01
Haridwar / Rishikesh — The Gateway to the Himalayas
Delhi / Dharamshala → Haridwar → Rishikesh

Your journey begins at Haridwar — one of the seven holiest cities in Hinduism, where the Ganges emerges from the Himalayas onto the plains. Har Ki Pauri ghat is the sacred bathing point where pilgrims immerse themselves in the river before the Yatra. In the evening, the Ganga Aarti is held here — a ritual of extraordinary beauty where priests wave large flames in synchronised patterns over the river as thousands of floating diyas illuminate the water. It is an experience that moves even the most composed traveller to silence.

Drive 25 km to Rishikesh for the night. Rishikesh — where the Beatles came to learn Transcendental Meditation in 1968 — is the world\'s yoga capital and a perfect spiritual preparation for the Char Dham ahead. Ram and Lakshman Jhula suspension bridges, the Parmarth Niketan ashram, and the evening Ganga Aarti here (different in character from Haridwar — more intimate, more contemplative) set the tone beautifully.

Har Ki Pauri, Haridwar Ganga Aarti Rishikesh Ram Jhula
Day02
Yamunotri — First Dham
Rishikesh → Barkot → Janki Chatti → Yamunotri

Depart at 5 AM for Janki Chatti (215 km from Rishikesh, 6 hours). From Janki Chatti, a 5 km trek (or pony/doli ride) leads to Yamunotri temple at 3,291 metres — the source of the sacred Yamuna River and the first dham of the Char Dham circuit. The temple houses the goddess Yamuna in a black marble idol, and the natural hot springs here are used to cook rice and potatoes as prasad — a remarkable tradition unique to this shrine. Return to Barkot for the night.

Yamunotri Temple 3,291m Surya Kund Hot Springs Barkot Night Halt
Day03
Gangotri — Second Dham
Barkot → Uttarkashi → Gangotri

Drive to Gangotri (100 km from Barkot, 4 hours) — the temple town at 3,100 metres near the source of the holy Ganges. The Gangotri temple faces the Bhagirathi River, which is the Ganges in its early course before it becomes the great river of the plains. The dramatic gorge setting — sheer rock walls, glacial blue water, forests of Deodar cedar — makes Gangotri one of the most visually dramatic of all four dhams. Perform puja at the temple and take a dip in the sacred kund. Stay overnight in Gangotri or return to Uttarkashi.

Gangotri Temple 3,100m Bhagirathi River Overnight Uttarkashi
Day04-05
Kedarnath — Third Dham (2 Days)
Uttarkashi → Guptkashi → Gaurikund → Kedarnath

Drive to Gaurikund (220 km from Uttarkashi via Rudraprayag, approximately 7 hours) — the road-end for Kedarnath. From Gaurikund, the 16 km trek (or helicopter) ascends to Kedarnath temple at 3,583 metres, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and the most dramatically situated temple in the Himalayas.

The approach to Kedarnath through the Mandakini Valley — past waterfalls, glaciers, and the backdrop of the Kedarnath and Bharat Khunta peaks — is a pilgrimage in itself. Evening aarti at the temple, with mountains turning gold and then purple in the fading light, is a transcendent experience. Stay overnight at Kedarnath for morning darshan before descending to Guptkashi or Phata for the second night.

Gaurikund Road-end Kedarnath Temple 3,583m Overnight at Kedarnath Morning Abhishek Puja
Day06-07
Badrinath — Fourth & Final Dham
Guptkashi → Joshimath → Badrinath

The final and most sacred of the four dhams. Badrinath temple at 3,133 metres sits on the banks of the Alaknanda River, flanked by the Nar and Narayan mountain ranges, with the Neelkanth Peak rising dramatically behind. Dedicated to Lord Vishnu, it is one of the 108 Divya Desams and draws hundreds of thousands of pilgrims annually.

The Badrinath-Kedarnath circuit (the do-dham) is the heart of the Char Dham pilgrimage. After puja and darshan at Badrinath, visit the Tapt Kund hot springs, Brahma Kapal (ritual for ancestors), and the Mana village — the last Indian village before the Tibetan border, just 3 km from Badrinath. Return to Joshimath for the night, then begin the homeward journey via Rishikesh and Haridwar.

Badrinath Temple 3,133m Tapt Kund Hot Springs Mana Village Joshimath Overnight
Day08-10
Return via Rishikesh & Haridwar
Joshimath → Devprayag → Rishikesh → Haridwar → Home

The return journey follows the Alaknanda and Ganges rivers downstream — stopping at the five sacred Prayags (river confluences) that are each considered holy sites in their own right. Devprayag, where the Bhagirathi and Alaknanda unite to form the Ganges proper, is particularly sacred and offers spectacular views from the confluence point.

Return to Rishikesh for an optional final day of yoga, meditation, or white-water rafting on the Ganges. Final overnight in Haridwar with one last morning dip at Har Ki Pauri before your driver takes you to Delhi, Dehradun Airport, or Dharamshala to complete your sacred North India journey.

Top Destinations in Uttarakhand

From sacred river banks to tiger reserves — Uttarakhand\'s diversity is extraordinary.

Yoga Capital

Rishikesh

Where the Ganges leaves the Himalayas, Rishikesh is the world\'s yoga and meditation capital. Ram Jhula, Lakshman Jhula, ashrams, evening Ganga Aarti, white-water rafting, bungee jumping, and cliff camping — spiritual depth and adventure perfectly combined.

Tip: Evening Aarti at Parmarth Niketan (6:30 PM) is more intimate than Haridwar. Yoga and meditation sessions available at dozens of reputable ashrams — book in advance.
Sacred City

Haridwar — Har Ki Pauri

One of India\'s seven sacred cities, Haridwar is the Ganges\' first contact with the plains. Har Ki Pauri ghat at dusk during the Ganga Aarti — with hundreds of floating oil lamps and thousands of pilgrims — is one of the most profoundly moving spectacles in all of India.

Tip: Arrive by 5 PM for the Ganga Aarti (6 PM in winter, 7 PM in summer). Leave valuables secured in the vehicle — the ghat gets very crowded.
Hill Station

Mussoorie

Perched at 2,005 metres above Dehradun, the "Queen of Hills" offers colonial-era charm, the famous Mall Road, Kempty Falls, and panoramic views of the Doon Valley and distant Himalayan peaks. A popular family destination easily combined with Haridwar and Rishikesh.

Tip: The George Everest house at the quiet end of Mussoorie has the finest views in town — and almost no tourists. Our drivers know the way.
Lake Town

Nainital

Centred around the crescent-shaped Naini Lake, Nainital is one of India\'s most picturesque hill stations. Boating on the lake, cable car rides to Snow View Point (2,270m), and walks along the Mall Road through pine-forested hills make it an ideal family retreat.

Tip: Visit on weekdays if possible — weekends are very crowded. The sunrise cable car ride to Snow View Point at 6:30 AM with no crowds is spectacular.
Wildlife Safari

Jim Corbett National Park

India\'s oldest national park, established in 1936, is home to 215+ Bengal tigers and offers some of Asia\'s finest wildlife safaris. The Dhikala zone — accessible only with advance permit — is where tiger sightings are most frequent, especially near the Ramganga River.

Tip: Dhikala zone requires advance permit booking through the park\'s online portal. We coordinate timing perfectly and know the best safari zones for different seasons.
UNESCO Heritage

Valley of Flowers

A UNESCO World Heritage Site in the Nanda Devi Biosphere Reserve, the Valley of Flowers blooms with over 500 species of alpine wildflowers from July to September. Our driver takes you to Govindghat, from where a 13 km trek leads to this extraordinary Himalayan meadow.

Tip: Open July 15 to October 5 only. August is peak bloom. Combine with Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara (4,300m) — one of the world\'s highest Sikh shrines — 6 km further.

Uttarakhand Taxi — Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the full Char Dham Yatra take?
The complete Char Dham Yatra (Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, Badrinath) comfortably takes 10-12 days from Haridwar. A faster circuit can be done in 8-9 days, but we recommend 10-12 days to avoid rushing the spiritual experience at each shrine.
When is Char Dham Yatra season?
Char Dham Yatra season runs May to October/November. Shrines open in late April or May (exact dates announced by Hindu calendar) and close after Diwali. May and September-October are the most comfortable months. June-September is monsoon — roads can be affected by landslides but shrines remain open.
Is taxi the best way to do Char Dham Yatra?
For families and groups, a private taxi (Innova Crysta) is absolutely the best option — complete flexibility to stop at any point, no shared vehicle discomfort, and a dedicated driver who knows all the routes and timing. For groups of 10+, Tempo Travellers provide group cohesion at an affordable per-person cost.
How do I reach Kedarnath?
Our taxi takes you to Gaurikund (the road-end at 1,982 metres). From Gaurikund, a 16 km trek or helicopter service (separate booking, subject to weather) reaches Kedarnath temple at 3,583 metres. We coordinate your trek or helicopter booking timing and wait at Gaurikund for your return.
How far is Rishikesh from Haridwar?
Rishikesh is just 22 km from Haridwar, approximately 45 minutes by taxi. We cover both on the same day for pilgrims arriving in Haridwar — Haridwar Ganga Aarti in the evening and Rishikesh exploration the next morning is the perfect combination.
Is Jim Corbett accessible year-round?
Jim Corbett is open October to June. The Dhikala zone (highest tiger density) is closed June 15 to November 15. The Bijrani and Jhirna zones are open year-round. We arrange all park permits and safari bookings on request.
How far is Dharamshala from Haridwar?
Haridwar is approximately 300 km from Dharamshala, a 5-6 hour drive. Many travellers combine a Himachal Pradesh trip with Uttarakhand pilgrimage — we make this seamlessly possible as a single service.
What vehicle is best for Char Dham Yatra?
Toyota Innova Crysta is our strongest recommendation. Its powerful diesel engine, high ground clearance, spacious interior, and luggage space make it ideal for the mountain roads of the Char Dham circuit. For groups of 10+, Tempo Travellers maintain group comfort and cohesion.
Can we visit Valley of Flowers from Rishikesh?
Yes. Our taxi takes you from Rishikesh to Govindghat (270 km, 8 hours) — the trek starting point for Valley of Flowers (13 km, 5 hours one way). We recommend 2 nights in the area to combine Valley of Flowers with Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara at 4,300 metres.
Can I combine Nainital and Jim Corbett?
Yes. Nainital and Jim Corbett are just 65 km apart and make an excellent 4-5 day Kumaon circuit. We cover both in a single trip with comfortable accommodation arrangements available on request.
Is airport pickup available in Uttarakhand?
Yes. We provide 24/7 pickup from Jolly Grant Airport (Dehradun) to Haridwar, Rishikesh, Mussoorie and onward, and from Pantnagar Airport to Nainital and Jim Corbett. Both airports are covered with flight-tracking for any delays.
