Where the Himalayas Meet the Soul

Dharamshala is not just a destination — it is an experience that quietly rearranges your priorities.

Cradled beneath the snow-dusted Dhauladhar range in the Kangra Valley, Dharamshala has captivated travellers for decades. Its upper reaches — known as McLeodganj — serve as the home of His Holiness the Dalai Lama and the Tibetan government in exile, which has given this small Himachali town a rare cosmopolitan energy you will find nowhere else in the mountains.

Walking through McLeodganj feels like stepping into two worlds simultaneously. One moment you are admiring a colonial-era stone church built by the British; the next you are circling a gleaming white stupa draped in prayer flags while Tibetan monks debate philosophy in the courtyard of the Tsuglagkhang Complex. Bakeries serving butter tea and momos sit alongside cafés playing Bob Dylan. Yoga studios, meditation retreats, and craft shops fill every lane.

Beyond the town, the real magic begins. Trek to Triund and wake to a panorama of jagged Himalayan peaks turning pink at dawn. Follow the forest path to Bhagsunag's twin-tiered waterfall. Drive through Naddi for the most photogenic sunset views of the Dhauladhar range. Head to Palampur to walk through the highest-altitude tea estates in India.

Whether you arrive to trek, meditate, explore Tibetan culture, or simply breathe mountain air — Dharamshala will offer more than you expect.

Sample Plan

A Perfect 4-Day Dharamshala Journey

This itinerary is a starting point. Every trip we plan is customised around your interests, budget, and pace of travel.

Day01
Arrival & First Impressions of McLeodganj
Gaggal Airport / Pathankot → McLeodganj

Your RJ Taxi driver will meet you at Gaggal Airport or Pathankot Railway Station holding a name board — no hunting around, no confusion. As you wind up the mountain road past Dharamshala town and into the pine-scented air of McLeodganj, your driver will point out landmarks and answer any questions about the region.

After checking in to your hotel, stroll down to the Tsuglagkhang Complex — the Dalai Lama Temple — in the afternoon. The complex houses a beautiful 20-foot golden Buddha and is usually open to visitors for meditation and exploration. In the evening, wander McLeodganj's main square, browse Tibetan handicraft shops, and settle into a rooftop café for your first Himalayan sunset. The mountains glow amber then deep rose before fading into a sky full of stars.

Gaggal Airport Pickup Tsuglagkhang Complex McLeodganj Market
Day02
Bhagsunag, Dharamkot & Triund Base
McLeodganj → Bhagsunag → Dharamkot → Gallu Devi

Start your morning with a drive to Bhagsunag — a peaceful temple complex with a natural spring pool, followed by a short walk up to the two-tiered waterfall that tumbles dramatically down a rocky gorge. The path is shaded by oak and rhododendron trees that burst into red bloom in spring.

From Bhagsunag, your taxi takes you to Dharamkot — a quieter, more artistic village just above McLeodganj that is beloved by long-term travellers, yoga students, and musicians. The air here is cool and clean, with views stretching across the entire Kangra Valley on clear days.

In the afternoon, drive up to Gallu Devi Temple — the starting point for the legendary Triund trek. Even if you do not trek, the short walk to Gallu temple offers breathtaking views of the Dhauladhar peaks. Trekkers can camp at Triund (3 km, 2-hour hike) and be picked up the next morning. Conclude your day with dinner at a Tibetan restaurant in McLeodganj — try the thukpa or steamed momos.

Bhagsunag Falls Dharamkot Village Gallu Devi Temple Triund Base (Optional Trek)
Day03
Naddi, St. John's Church & Kangra Fort
McLeodganj → Naddi → St. John's → Kangra

Begin with a visit to Naddi Viewpoint, located 6 km above McLeodganj. On clear mornings, the unobstructed panorama of the Dhauladhar range — including the pyramidal peak of Lahesh Cave towering at 5,200 metres — is simply breathtaking. Morning is the ideal time to photograph the peaks before afternoon haze sets in.

Next, descend through the forest to St. John in the Wilderness — a Gothic-style Anglican church built in 1852, surrounded by a quiet graveyard shaded by ancient deodar trees. The solitude of the spot and the ivy-covered stone walls make it one of the most atmospheric places in Dharamshala. There is something deeply moving about standing in this jungle clearing, surrounded by the sounds of birdsong and mountain wind.

In the afternoon, drive down to Kangra town to explore the massive Kangra Fort — one of the oldest and largest forts in the Himalayas — and the beautifully carved Brajeshwari Devi Temple. Cap the day with a stroll through Dharamshala's cricket stadium, one of the most scenic cricket venues in the world.

Naddi Viewpoint St. John's Church Kangra Fort Brajeshwari Temple HPCA Cricket Stadium
Day04
Palampur Tea Gardens & Departure
McLeodganj → Palampur → Airport/Station

Before your departure, take a morning drive to Palampur — a charming town 40 km from Dharamshala, set at the foothills of the Dhauladhar range and famous for its vast Kangra Tea estates. Walking through the terraced rows of dark green tea bushes, with the mountains reflected in their polished leaves, is one of those quietly beautiful experiences that stays with you long after you return home.

Visit the Bundla Tea Factory for a guided tour explaining how Kangra tea — one of India's most prized — is grown, harvested, withered, rolled, and dried. Buy some directly from the factory before you leave. Stop at Chamunda Devi Temple on the return route, then your RJ Taxi driver will take you comfortably to Gaggal Airport or Pathankot Railway Station in time for your onward journey.

Must-Visit

Top Attractions in Dharamshala & McLeodganj

Our drivers take you to every iconic spot — and the hidden gems most tourists never find.

Spiritual

Tsuglagkhang Complex

The spiritual centre of Tibetan Buddhism in exile. Home of the Dalai Lama, this complex houses the Namgyal Monastery, a museum of Tibetan history, and the stunning 20-foot Shakyamuni Buddha statue. Monks in maroon robes chant, debate, and meditate here daily.

Tip: Arrive early morning (7–9 AM) for a quieter experience. Photography is welcome outside but restricted inside prayer halls.
Adventure

Triund — The Crown of Dharamshala

At 2,875 metres, the Triund ridge offers one of the most accessible high-altitude camping experiences in India. The 9 km trail from Gallu Devi Temple passes through oak-rhododendron forest and opens onto a sweeping meadow with 180-degree views of the Dhauladhar and Kangra Valley.

Tip: We drop you at Gallu Devi Temple and pick you up next morning. Carry warm layers — temperatures drop sharply after sunset even in summer.
Nature

Bhagsunag Waterfall

A 20-minute walk from McLeodganj leads to the twin cascades of Bhagsunag Falls, tumbling down black granite rocks into a crystal pool. The ancient Bhagsunath Shiva temple at the base is one of the oldest in Kangra district, drawing both pilgrims and tourists.

Tip: Visit on weekdays to avoid weekend crowds. The walk up is easy and suitable for all fitness levels.
Viewpoint

Naddi Viewpoint

Perched 6 km above McLeodganj, Naddi offers arguably the finest panorama of the Dhauladhar range. The small village is home to several yoga centres and guesthouses. On clear days, you can see peaks over 5,000 metres from the roadside viewpoint.

Tip: Come at sunrise or just before sunset for golden hour photography. The Dal Lake below Naddi is worth a short detour.
Heritage

St. John in the Wilderness

Built in 1852 by British colonials, this Gothic stone church stands in a quiet forest clearing 3 km from McLeodganj. The stained glass windows were donated by Lady Elgin in memory of Lord Elgin, the 8th Viceroy of India, who is buried here. A deeply atmospheric colonial-era gem.

Tip: The surrounding graveyard and forest path make for wonderful walks. Combine with a visit to Dharamkot village nearby.
Cultural

Palampur Tea Gardens

Located 40 km from Dharamshala, Palampur is India's northernmost tea-growing region. The terraced estates produce the rare Kangra Tea — a light, delicate brew once exported to Russia. Walking through rows of manicured tea bushes with the Dhauladhar as backdrop is utterly serene.

Tip: Visit the Bundla Tea Factory for a free guided tour and buy fresh estate tea directly from the factory at best prices.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before booking your Dharamshala taxi.

Which is the best time to visit Dharamshala?
March to June offers pleasant weather (15–28°C) ideal for trekking and sightseeing. September to November brings crystal-clear skies after monsoon. December to February is cold (0–10°C) but snowfall is possible — magical but roads can be challenging. July–August is monsoon season with lush greenery but occasional road closures.
Is airport pickup available from Gaggal Airport?
Absolutely. We provide 24/7 pickup from Gaggal (Kangra) Airport, which is 15 km from Dharamshala and 18 km from McLeodganj. We track your flight and adjust pickup time if there are delays — no extra charges.
Can I trek to Triund and get a taxi pick-up the next morning?
Yes! This is one of our most popular services. We drop you at Gallu Devi Temple, you trek up to Triund and camp overnight, and we pick you up the following morning at a pre-arranged time. Just WhatsApp us when you're heading back down.
Do your drivers speak English?
Yes. Most of our drivers are comfortable in English, especially for common traveller needs like navigating, restaurant recommendations, sightseeing tips, and general conversation. For foreign tourists, we can request our most fluent English-speaking drivers.
Can I book a one-way taxi from Delhi to Dharamshala?
Yes, we offer one-way taxis from Delhi, Chandigarh, Amritsar, and all major North Indian cities to Dharamshala and McLeodganj. The Delhi–Dharamshala drive is approximately 8 hours and we recommend an early morning departure to enjoy the mountain scenery during daylight.

Yes, we cover all major outstation routes — Dharamshala to Manali, Shimla, Dalhousie, Chandigarh, Delhi, Amritsar, Haridwar, and beyond. All inter-state permits are handled by us. Contact us with your route for a custom quote.
