Valley of the Gods

Where Every Road Leads to a New Wonder

Manali does not reveal itself all at once. It unfolds — one valley, one pass, one river bend at a time.

Cradled between the Beas River and the towering Pir Panjal range at 2,050 metres, Manali is Himachal Pradesh\'s adventure capital and one of India\'s most beloved honeymoon destinations. But beyond its popular identity lies a destination of extraordinary depth — ancient temples, apple orchards, monasteries perched on cliffsides, roaring rivers ideal for white-water rafting, and ski slopes that come alive in winter.

The drive to Manali from Dharamshala alone is worth the journey. The route follows the Beas River through the Kullu Valley — past silver-roofed temples, terraced apple farms and pine-covered hillsides that shift in colour with every season. By the time you arrive in Manali town and catch your first glimpse of the snow-streaked Rohtang Pass from the main street, you will already understand why people keep returning year after year.

With the Atal Tunnel now connecting Manali to Lahaul Spiti year-round — a 9.02 km engineering marvel at 3,000 metres — there is more to explore than ever before. Beyond the tunnel lies the cold desert landscape of Sissu, the sacred monasteries of Lahaul, and the high-altitude meadows that remain untouched by the mainstream tourist trail.

A Perfect 5-Day Manali Journey

A flexible starting point — every trip is custom-built around your group, dates, and interests.

Day01
Dharamshala to Manali — The Great Mountain Drive
Dharamshala / Delhi → Manali

Your RJ Taxi driver arrives early at your hotel in Dharamshala (or picks you up from Delhi/Chandigarh if you prefer). The journey to Manali takes you through the spectacular Kullu Valley — following the Beas River north as it flows silver through orchards heavy with apples in autumn and cherry blossoms in spring.

The road passes through Mandi — an ancient temple town worth a short stop — then climbs through Kullu town and enters the valley proper. Your driver will point out the best chai stops and viewpoints en route. By late afternoon, as you enter Manali and your first view of the snow-streaked peaks appears between pine trees, the journey will already feel worth every kilometre. Check in, rest, and explore Manali\'s Old Town in the evening — the 450-year-old Hadimba Devi Temple is just minutes away.

Mandi Town Stop Kullu Valley Drive Manali Hotel Check-in Old Manali Evening Walk
Day02
Rohtang Pass — The Roof of the Road
Manali → Rohtang Pass (3,978m) → Return

An early 6 AM start is essential for Rohtang — the pass gets crowded by midday and afternoon weather can turn unpredictably. Your driver handles the NGT permit paperwork in advance. The 51 km drive from Manali climbs through increasingly dramatic terrain — pine forests give way to rocky moonscapes, and then suddenly, the world goes white.

Rohtang Pass at 3,978 metres is an otherworldly experience. Even in summer, snow lies thick on either side of the road. The views over the Chandra Valley and the distant Lahaul peaks are staggering. After the pass, we descend slightly to the Chandra River for photographs before returning to Manali in time for a late lunch. Your driver knows all the best photography spots that most tourists drive straight past.

Rohtang Pass 3,978m Chandra Valley View Snow Activities
Day03
Solang Valley, Atal Tunnel & Sissu
Manali → Solang → Atal Tunnel → Sissu

Solang Valley, just 14 km from Manali, is the adventure hub of the region. In winter it becomes a ski resort; in summer it offers paragliding, zorbing, quad biking, and a spectacular cable car ride to 2,480 metres with panoramic views of the surrounding peaks.

From Solang, continue to the Atal Tunnel — the world\'s longest highway tunnel above 10,000 feet, at 9.02 km. The eight-minute drive through this engineering marvel emerges into an entirely different world: the cold desert of Lahaul. Sissu village on the other side sits beside a turquoise glacial lake with a waterfall cascading down bare rocky cliffs — a scene so dramatically different from the green Manali valley that it almost feels like crossing into another country. Return to Manali via the tunnel for an evening visit to Hadimba Temple.

Solang Valley Adventure Atal Tunnel (9.02 km) Sissu Lake, Lahaul Hadimba Devi Temple
Day04
Kasol, Manikaran & Parvati Valley
Manali → Kasol → Manikaran → Return

The Parvati Valley day trip is one of the most loved detours from Manali. The 90 km journey takes you south and then east into the mystical Parvati Valley — a world unto itself, known equally for its natural beauty and its spiritual energy.

Kasol is a riverside village that has become a backpacker hub, famous for its Israeli cafes, reggae music, and the crystal-clear Parvati River running beside the main street. Spend an hour walking the river bank and having lunch at one of the legendary cafes. Then continue 5 km to Manikaran — one of Sikhism\'s holiest pilgrimage sites, famous for its bubbling hot springs (so hot they are used to cook langar prasad), and the sacred Gurudwara Manikaran Sahib built over the springs. The Shiva temple complex here, with its dramatic rock formations and natural steam vents, is extraordinary. Return to Manali by evening through the pine-forested valley road.

Kasol Riverside Manikaran Hot Springs Gurudwara Manikaran Sahib Parvati Valley Drive
Day05
Naggar Castle, Jagatsukh & Departure
Manali → Naggar → Bhuntar Airport / Onwards

Your final morning takes you south through the lesser-explored but beautiful old Kullu Valley road to Naggar — the ancient capital of Kullu kingdom. Naggar Castle, a magnificent 1,500-year-old stone-and-wood structure, now a heritage hotel, sits on a hillside with some of the finest valley views in the region. Visit the Nicholas Roerich Art Gallery within the castle grounds — the Russian painter spent his final years here and created some of his greatest Himalayan works.

On the return, stop at Jagatsukh — one of the oldest villages in the valley, with intricately carved stone temples dedicated to Shiva and Sandhya Gayatri, mostly unvisited by tourists. Your driver then delivers you comfortably to Bhuntar Airport (Kullu) or continues back to Dharamshala/Delhi depending on your onward journey.

Top Attractions in & Around Manali

From snow peaks to spiritual sanctuaries — Manali packs extraordinary variety into a compact region.

Adventure

Rohtang Pass (3,978m)

The dramatic gateway to Lahaul Spiti, Rohtang sits at 3,978 metres and offers a snow-covered landscape even in June. The views of the Chandra Valley and Kullu Valley from the top are among the most spectacular in all of Himachal Pradesh.

Tip: Open May-October only. NGT permits required — we arrange these. Depart by 6 AM to avoid crowds and afternoon weather.
Adventure

Solang Valley

Himachal\'s adventure epicentre, 14 km from Manali. Winter brings skiing, snowboarding and snow-scooter rides. Summer offers paragliding, zorbing, quad bikes and a stunning cable car ascending to 2,480 metres above a green valley.

Tip: Paragliding is best in the morning when winds are calmer. Book activities on-site — no advance booking needed for most.
Engineering Marvel

Atal Tunnel & Sissu

At 9.02 km, the world\'s longest highway tunnel above 10,000 feet burrows through the Pir Panjal range, emerging into the surreal cold-desert landscape of Lahaul. Sissu village on the other end has a glacial lake and waterfall that looks like another planet.

Tip: Open year-round unlike Rohtang Pass. A brilliant winter excursion when Rohtang is closed — Sissu receives far less snow.
Spiritual

Hadimba Devi Temple

Set in a cedar forest at the edge of Manali town, this 450-year-old pagoda-style wooden temple dedicated to Hadimba — wife of Bhima from the Mahabharata — is one of the most architecturally unique temples in North India. The four-storey wooden tower is extraordinary.

Tip: Visit early morning before tour groups arrive. The forest walk from the temple to the Van Vihar park is peaceful and beautiful.
Bohemian

Kasol & Parvati Valley

The backpacker\'s paradise of the Himalayas. Kasol sits where the Parvati River meets the highway, lined with cafes serving Israeli food, fresh juices and mountain views. The entire Parvati Valley stretching beyond to Kheerganga is hauntingly beautiful.

Tip: Kheerganga (a 12 km trek from Barshaini) has natural hot springs at 2,950m. Our driver can drop at Barshaini and pick up next morning.
Heritage

Naggar Castle

This 1,500-year-old stone castle perched above the Kullu Valley was the royal seat of the Kullu kingdom for 1,400 years. Today a heritage hotel, its corridors contain ancient wooden galleries, stone carvings, and views that stopped Nicholas Roerich in his tracks when he chose it as his final home.

Tip: The Roerich Art Gallery here is underrated and often empty of tourists. Allow 90 minutes to explore the castle and gallery properly.

FAQ

Manali Taxi — Frequently Asked Questions

Which vehicle is best for a Manali trip?
The Toyota Innova Crysta is our strongest recommendation for Manali — especially for routes including Rohtang Pass. Its powerful diesel engine, high ground clearance, and spacious interior make it ideal for mountain roads with luggage. For groups of 8-16, the Tempo Traveller is excellent.
Is Rohtang Pass open all year?
Rohtang Pass is open approximately May to late October, subject to weather and snowfall. However, the Atal Tunnel now provides year-round access to Lahaul Spiti — so winter trips to Sissu and Lahaul are fully possible even when Rohtang is closed.
Do you handle the Rohtang Pass NGT permit?
Yes, we handle all NGT (National Green Tribunal) permits required for Rohtang Pass. Permits are obtained in advance and included in your trip planning. There is an additional government permit fee payable at actuals.
What is the best time to visit Manali for snowfall?
December to February brings heavy snowfall directly in Manali town — creating a winter wonderland. For Rohtang Pass snow, visit May-June (the pass reopens) or late September-October (just before it closes). January is the snowiest month in Manali itself.
How far is Kasol from Manali and is it worth a day trip?
Kasol is about 90 km from Manali (2 hours). It is absolutely worth a full-day trip, combining Kasol riverside with Manikaran hot springs and Gurudwara. We depart early morning and return by evening with stops at key spots along the Parvati Valley.
Can we go from Manali to Spiti Valley?
Yes! The Manali-Spiti route via Atal Tunnel and onward to Kaza is one of the most spectacular drives in India. It requires a minimum of 3-4 days and an SUV. We organise full Spiti Valley circuits — contact us with your dates for a detailed plan.
How far is Manali from Dharamshala by taxi?
Manali is approximately 250 km from Dharamshala, a scenic 5-6 hour drive following the Beas River through the Kullu Valley. We offer both direct transfers and leisure drives with stops at scenic viewpoints along the way.
Is taxi available from Delhi to Manali?
Yes. Delhi to Manali is approximately 550 km, a 12-14 hour drive depending on traffic. We recommend overnight drives (departure around 9-10 PM) arriving at Manali by early morning — a popular option for long-weekend trips from Delhi.
Are your drivers experienced on snow-covered roads?
Absolutely. Our drivers have years of experience on high-altitude mountain roads including Rohtang Pass in snow conditions. We also equip vehicles with snow chains during winter months for added safety on icy roads.
Can I customise my Manali itinerary?
Yes — in fact, we encourage it. Every Manali trip we operate is tailored to your group\'s interests, pace, and priorities. Whether you want to focus on adventure, photography, spirituality, or just leisurely sightseeing — we build the perfect plan.
Is there airport pickup from Bhuntar (Kullu) Airport?
Yes. Bhuntar Airport (also called Kullu-Manali Airport) is 52 km from Manali. We provide 24/7 pickup from Bhuntar directly to Manali, Kasol, or onward to Dharamshala. We track your flight for any delays.
