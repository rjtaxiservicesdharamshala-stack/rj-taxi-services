Shimla — A Hill Station That Never Goes Out of Style

Shimla carries its colonial past with quiet elegance, wearing it like a well-tailored coat draped over Himalayan shoulders.

The summer capital of British India, Shimla was the administrative nerve centre of the Raj from 1864 to 1939. Walking its famous Mall Road today, you can still feel the echo of another era — in the Victorian spires of Christ Church, the Edwardian columns of the Gaiety Theatre, and the ivy-draped facades of Gorton Castle. Shimla has preserved this architectural heritage while evolving into a vibrant, modern hill station that draws millions of visitors every year.

What makes Shimla endlessly rewarding is its variety. The Mall Road and Ridge offer colonial architecture and panoramic views. Kufri, 16 km away, brings snow activities and yak rides in winter, and lush meadows in summer. Chail at 2,250 metres holds the world\'s highest cricket ground. Mashobra\'s forested trails are peaceful escapes from the tourist circuit. And Naldehra\'s golf course — established by Lord Curzon in 1905 — remains one of the most scenic in Asia.

Our taxi service connects you seamlessly from Dharamshala (5 hours), Chandigarh (3 hours), or Delhi (7 hours) and handles all local sightseeing with experienced mountain drivers who know every curve and viewpoint.

A Perfect 4-Day Shimla Experience

Covering Shimla\'s colonial heritage, Kufri snow, Chail palace and Naldehra\'s peace — customised for your group.

Day01
Arrival & Shimla Colonial Heritage
Dharamshala / Chandigarh → Shimla

Your RJ Taxi driver picks you up from Dharamshala, Chandigarh, or the nearest airport. The journey to Shimla from Chandigarh (3 hours) follows NH-5 through increasingly dramatic mountain terrain before arriving at the colonial capital perched on seven hills.

After checking in, begin your Shimla exploration on foot along the Mall Road — the 1.3 km pedestrian promenade lined with Victorian-era buildings, shops, and cafes. Walk up to the Ridge for the best panoramic view of the town and the snow-streaked Himalayan range beyond. Visit Christ Church — the second oldest church in North India — whose stained glass windows depicting faith, hope, charity, and fortitude are particularly beautiful in afternoon light.

Mall Road The Ridge Christ Church Gaiety Theatre
Day02
Kufri — Snow, Valleys & Himalayan Views
Shimla → Kufri → Fagu → Return

Depart Shimla at 7 AM to reach Kufri before the crowds. At 2,600 metres, Kufri sits 16 km from Shimla and is famous for snow sports in winter and horse riding with panoramic views in summer. The Himalayan Wildlife Zoo at Kufri houses snow leopards, Himalayan bears, musk deer, and Tibetan wolves — a unique high-altitude zoo experience.

Continue to Fagu — a quiet viewpoint 8 km beyond Kufri where the valley views are arguably even more spectacular than Kufri itself, yet far fewer tourists visit. On clear days, you can see the Chail Palace ridge in one direction and the entire Shimla valley in the other. Lunch at a local dhaba in Kufri before returning to Shimla in the afternoon.

Kufri Meadow Himalayan Zoo Fagu Viewpoint Snow Activities
Day03
Chail Palace & Naldehra Golf Course
Shimla → Chail → Naldehra → Mashobra

Chail was built by Maharaja Bhupinder Singh of Patiala in 1891 after he was banned from Shimla by the British Viceroy. He simply built his own summer capital — higher than Shimla, grander than Shimla, and featuring the world\'s highest cricket ground (2,444 metres) that still hosts matches today. The Chail Palace, now a heritage hotel, is a stunning Victorian building set among giant deodar trees. The drive from Shimla (45 km) passes through Kufri and descends dramatically into the lush Chail valley.

After Chail, drive to Naldehra — a serene plateau 22 km from Shimla where Lord Curzon established Asia\'s oldest golf course in 1905. Even non-golfers find the setting magical — a rolling green course framed by cedar forests and Himalayan peaks. The famous Naldehra Temple fair (June) is one of Himachal\'s most colourful events. Return via Mashobra — a quiet forested village 13 km from Shimla perfect for evening walks.

Chail Palace Highest Cricket Ground Naldehra Golf Course Mashobra Forest
Day04
Jakhu Temple, Jakhoo Hill & Departure
Shimla → Jakhu → Chandigarh / Dharamshala

Begin your final morning with a visit to Jakhu Temple — dedicated to Lord Hanuman and perched at 2,455 metres on Jakhu Hill, the highest point of Shimla. The 108-foot statue of Hanuman visible from much of Shimla is a modern landmark, but the ancient temple dates back to the Ramayana era according to legend. The path up to Jakhu is populated with resident monkeys — entertaining but respectful of space.

The views from Jakhu Hill are arguably the finest in Shimla — a 360-degree panorama of forested ridges, the town below, and on clear winter days, the full sweep of the greater Himalayan range to the north. Your driver will take you comfortably back to Chandigarh, Dharamshala, or the nearest airport depending on your onward journey.

Must-Visit

Top Attractions in & Around Shimla

From snowy Kufri to the colonial Ridge — our drivers know every highlight and hidden gem.

Heritage

Mall Road & The Ridge

The beating heart of Shimla — a 1.3 km vehicle-free promenade lined with Victorian buildings, cafes, and shops. The Ridge connects to the larger open space offering panoramic views of surrounding peaks and the famous Christ Church silhouette.

Tip: Visit early morning before shops open for the most atmospheric photography with empty streets and morning mountain light.
Snow & Adventure

Kufri Snow Point

16 km from Shimla at 2,600 metres, Kufri is famous for snow in winter and panoramic views year-round. Horse riding on snow, skiing lessons, and the Himalayan Wildlife Zoo make it a full-day activity in itself.

Tip: January-February for guaranteed snow activities. Visit at 7-8 AM before tourist rush. Carry warm layers even in summer.
Royal Heritage

Chail Palace

Built by Maharaja Bhupinder Singh above Shimla at 2,250 metres, Chail has the world\'s highest cricket ground and a magnificent Victorian palace now operating as a heritage hotel. The drive through deodar forest from Kufri to Chail is stunning.

Tip: Combine Chail with Kufri and Naldehra in a single day for maximum efficiency. Allow 1.5 hours minimum at the palace grounds.
Scenic

Naldehra Golf Course

Established by Lord Curzon in 1905, this is Asia\'s oldest golf course — a beautifully maintained 9-hole course at 2,250 metres, surrounded by cedar and oak forests with mountain views. Non-golfers can walk the course perimeter for free.

Tip: The Naldehra Temple Fair (May/June) is one of Himachal\'s most colourful cultural events — worth timing your visit around.
Nature

Mashobra & Craignano

Just 13 km from Shimla, Mashobra is a peaceful forested retreat famous for the Craignano estate — a beautifully preserved British-era orchard estate now a HPTDC resort. The apple and cherry orchards in spring bloom are spectacular.

Tip: Visit in April for cherry blossoms or September-October for apple harvest. Combine with Wildflower Hall luxury resort for a special experience.
Spiritual

Jakhu Temple

The ancient Hanuman temple at 2,455 metres — the highest point of Shimla — is reached by a 2 km forest walk from the Mall. The 108-foot Hanuman statue is visible from across Shimla. The forest trail through resident monkeys is an adventure in itself.

Tip: Our driver drops at the base and waits. Carry a stick to deter the monkeys and do not display food. Allow 2 hours for the round trip.

Shimla Taxi — Frequently Asked Questions

How far is Shimla from Dharamshala by taxi?
Shimla is approximately 230 km from Dharamshala, a scenic 5-hour drive. The route passes through Mandi and follows beautiful mountain roads through the Beas and Sutlej valleys. We offer direct transfers and leisure drives with scenic stops.
Is Kufri good for snowfall? When does it snow in Kufri?
Kufri receives snowfall from December to February. January and February are the best months for guaranteed snow and snow activities. Even without snow, Kufri offers beautiful valley views and horse riding from March to November.
Can I visit Chail and Kufri in one day from Shimla?
Yes, a well-planned full-day circuit from Shimla can include Kufri, Fagu, Chail, and Naldehra. We recommend a 7 AM start. Our drivers optimise the route to minimise backtracking and maximise time at each location.
Is Shimla accessible year-round?
Yes, Shimla is accessible all year. Summer (April-June) is warm and crowded. Monsoon (July-September) is lush but can have landslides on some roads. Winter (December-February) brings snow and is popular for the winter experience. October-November offers the best weather with clear skies.
Is airport pickup available from Shimla (Jubbarhatti)?
Yes, we provide pickup from Jubbarhatti Airport (23 km from Shimla) to Shimla town and all surrounding areas. The narrow mountain road to the airport requires experienced drivers — ours navigate it daily.
What is the Kalka-Shimla toy train and can we arrange it?
The Kalka-Shimla narrow-gauge railway is a UNESCO World Heritage route — one of the most scenic train journeys in India. We can drop you at Kalka Station, you take the 5-hour heritage train to Shimla, and we meet you at Shimla Railway Station. A wonderful experience we strongly recommend.
Can I do a day trip to Shimla from Chandigarh?
Yes, Shimla is just 115 km and 3 hours from Chandigarh. We can take you for a day trip covering the Mall Road, Ridge, Jakhu Temple, and Kufri, returning to Chandigarh by evening. An early 5 AM departure maximises your time in Shimla.
Which areas of Shimla are restricted for vehicles?
The Mall Road is pedestrian-only. Our drivers know the designated parking areas and drop/pickup points for all major attractions. Visitors walk on the Mall Road while the vehicle waits — completely seamless with our experienced drivers.
Is there accommodation at Chail?
Yes, Chail Palace (now a heritage hotel) and several guesthouses offer accommodation. Staying in Chail for one night is a wonderful experience and we can arrange drop-off at Chail and pickup the next morning to continue your tour.
How do I book a Shimla taxi?
Simply WhatsApp or call us at +91-9317082083, or submit the enquiry form on this page. Share your travel dates, group size, and pickup location — we respond with a full transparent quote within 30 minutes.
Do you offer Delhi to Shimla taxi?
Yes. Delhi to Shimla is approximately 350 km (7 hours). We recommend departing Delhi by 4 AM to reach Shimla by late morning, avoiding the peak traffic period in Chandigarh. Overnight drives with early morning arrival are also available.
